TSP Webinar: Contributions and Funds

Posted Nov. 15

On Wednesday, Nov. 28 from 12 to 1:30 p.m., a representative from the Federal Retirement Thrift Investment Board will facilitate a webinar entitled: "TSP Contributions and Funds." If you are covered by the Federal Employees' Retirement System (FERS), the TSP is one part of your retirement package. If you are covered by the Civil Service Retirement System (CSRS), the TSP is a supplement to your CSRS annuity.

This course covers TSP contributions, funding of TSP and outlines several retirement scenarios to get you thinking about how to best turn your savings into income for the future.

For additional information, please contact Ramona Conway at conway.ramona@epa.gov or 202-564-7571.

People needing reasonable accommodations should contact Kristin P. Tropp at tropp.kristin@epa.gov or 202-559-0006.

OITA's Native American Heritage Month Event

Posted Nov. 14

Date: Nov. 19
Time: 12-1
Location: WJCE 1153

Come join the Office of International and Tribal Affairs as we host a special presentation from Dr. Karenne Wood (Monacan Indian Nation), in recognition of Native American Heritage Month!

Dr. Karenne Wood is an enrolled member of the Monacan Indian Nation, one of six tribes in Virginia to have received federal recognition status in January 2018. Dr. Wood is an anthropologist who directs the Virginia Indian Programs at the Virginia Foundation for the Humanities. Previously, she worked at the National Museum of the American Indian (NMAI) as a researcher and at the Association on American Indian Affairs as a repatriation specialist. During the event, Dr. Wood will offer her perspectives and ideas to expand the story about Virginia's first peoples.

All are welcome to join - we hope to see you there! For any questions about the event, reach out to st-denis.francine@epa.gov.

Get Your New EPASS Badge

Posted Oct. 30

All EPA personnel must have a new EPASS badge by Dec. 31; the new EPASS badges say, "EPA." After this date, your existing badge, which says,"US EPA," will no longer provide building access.

After receiving an email from HSPD12Admin notifying you to get enrolled for a new badge, don't wait. Follow the steps below to receive your EPASS badge:

  • Make an appointment at your local badging office, or visit one of the 200 USAccess enrollment sites near your work or home.
  • Bring two forms of ID with you to your appointment (driver's license and current EPASS badge are acceptable).
  • You will be notified when your badge is ready and directed where to pick it up.

If you have any questions, contact SmartCard@epa.gov or your local badging office.

New Contract for Sign Language Interpreting and Captioning Services

Posted Oct. 25

EPA awarded a new contract for sign language interpreting and Communication Access Real-Time Translation (CART)/captioning services to Access Interpreting. The Office of Human Resources centrally manages sign language interpreting and CART/captioning services for use by deaf and hard of hearing employees at EPA headquarters and for use by any headquarters program office to support internal or public events nationwide.

Regional offices can use the contract, which was competed and awarded as a blanket purchase agreement (BPA No.68HEOH18A0005) to obtain services to support their employees and to support their internal and public events at their discretion and at their own costs. However, the use of this contract by regional offices is not mandatory.

Access Interpreting offers the following services as part of the BPA:

  • Basic sign language.
  • Scientific sign language.
  • Video remote interpreting.
  • Computer access real-time translation (on-site and remote).

Additional information on the new sign language interpreting and CART/captioning contract can be found on the EPA intranet's Interpreter site or by emailing the OHR Interpreting mailbox at interpretingservices@epa.gov.

Health & Wellness

Defeat Depression

Posted Nov. 7

According to the Anxiety and Depression Association of America (ADAA), about 16.1 million American adults experienced at least one major depressive episode in the last year. Additionally, according to the ADAA, depression is the leading cause of disability in the United States among people ages 15 through 44. Symptoms of depression include persistent sadness, change in sleep patterns, loss of interest in hobbies, and thoughts of suicide. If you have symptoms of depression, there are steps you can take to help yourself:

  • See a mental health professional as soon as possible.
  • Confide in a trusted friend or family member.
  • Educate yourself about depression.
  • Recognize what triggers your symptoms.
  • Access EPA's Employee Assistance Program, which includes confidential counseling and referral services.
  • Use online lifestyle counseling resources.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
