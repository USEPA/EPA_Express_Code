Participants will learn about the benefits of a Mediterranean lifestyle including the traditional "Mediterranean Diet." This diet is characterized by high intake of healthy fats such as olive oil, fruits, nuts, vegetables, and lean meats; a moderate intake of fish and poultry; a low intake of dairy products, red meat, processed meats, and sugary sweets; and wine in moderation.

Sleep Well

Posted April 4

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, one-third of Americans report that they usually get less than the recommended amount of sleep. Insufficient sleep is linked to health complications, including chronic diseases such as diabetes, Heart disease, obesity, and depression.

  • Go to bed earlier to get at least seven hours of sleep.
  • Ensure that your bedroom is quiet, dark, and comfortable.
  • Avoid large meals and alcohol prior to going to sleep.
  • Reduce your caffeine intake, especially later in the day.
  • Manage your stress levels.
  • Avoid rigorous activities (e.g., exercising) in the evening.
  • Use a nighttime setting for your smartphone and other electronic devices before bed.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Defend Against Diabetes

Posted April 3

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, more than 30 million Americans are currently living with diabetes, and one in four cases is undiagnosed. Diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels, which increases the risk of developing heart disease or having a stroke.

Visit the American Diabetes Association website to take an online diabetes risk assessment for type 2 diabetes (Exit). If you score "at risk" for type 2 diabetes, follow up with your personal physician to schedule a blood glucose screening, and take steps to eat a balanced diet and maintain a healthy weight. You should also visit your personal physician at least once a year to monitor your health.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
