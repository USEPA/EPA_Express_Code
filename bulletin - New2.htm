2019 Bike to Work Week & Day!

Posted May 13

Gear up and get outside for National Bike to Work Week from May 13 to 17 and Bike to Work Day on Friday, May 17. Get yourself and your bike ready to make this healthy and sustainable activity a part of your commute!

  • Reduce: Did you know by leaving your car at home and biking to work just two days a week helps reduce carbon pollution by an average of two tons per year? (EPA's Bike to Work page.)
  • Reuse: Don't have a bicycle? Check out your local thrift store! Purchasing used items are often less expensive and just as good as new. Bike sharing (is another sustainable option. (Exit)
  • Let's Cycle: Riding a bicycle is a simple way to add exercise to your routine, and a (physically active) lifestyle can help you to prevent disease and maintain a healthy weight .
  • No matter where or when you ride a bicycle, stay safe on the road by always wearing a properly fitted helmet and obeying all traffic signs, signals, and lane markings.

For more information, visit the EPA's Bike to Work page on EPA's website.

Your health during National Women's Health Week

Posted May 10

Your health is a lifelong journey. It's unique. It's personal. It yours. This National Women's Health Week, learn what you can do to take the next step on your personal health journey.

Led by the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services' Office on Women’s Health (OWH), National Women's Health Week encourages women to prioritize their health and take simple steps to improve it. To be your healthiest self, you can:

  • Visit a health care provider for a well-woman visit (checkup) and preventive screenings and vaccines.
  • Get active.
  • Eat healthy.
  • Pay attention to your mental health, including getting enough sleep and managing stress.
  • Practice safe behaviors, such as quitting smoking, not texting while driving, and taking steps to protect yourself from sexually transmitted infections.

For ideas and tips to help you improve your health in a way that's uniquely you, visit www.womenshealth.gov/nwhw. Start working toward your health goals today - and share your progress with us using #NWHW and #FindYourHealth.

Be Your Best Without Stress

Posted April 15

Stress is a physical, emotional and behavioral reaction to change in a person's life. According to the Mayo Clinic, long-term stress can increase the risk of developing health problems, such as depression, heart disease, weight gain, and anxiety. To help combat stress:

  • Reflect on what you have accomplished at the end of the day instead of focusing on what you were unable to do.
  • Use effective planning to minimize the impact of potentially stressful situations.
  • Minimize contact with individuals who cause stress when possible.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle by eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and exercising regularly.
  • Take time to relax.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Eat Healthy, Be Healthy

Posted March 27

Healthy food choices and proper nutrition can help prevent obesity, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, cancer, and osteoporosis. To make healthy food choices:

  • Bring healthy, low-fat foods to meetings.
  • Explore the healthy food choices at your local snack bar and in vending machines.
  • Visit a local farmers' market for fresh produce to incorporate into your meals.
  • Join the Walk to Wellness and Step It Up Challenge to commit to healthy eating, regular physical activity and lifelong good habits.
