OIG Helps Address Workplace Violence

Posted Aug. 14

The Office of Inspector General (OIG) helps EPA implement its Preventing Violence in the Workplace policy by investigating allegations of assault or serious threats of violence in the EPA workplace. Anyone who experiences or witnesses workplace violence, as well as any supervisor who receives a report of workplace violence, should immediately:

  • Contact the most readily available law enforcement authority to ensure no further violence occurs.
  • Ensure that any employee who has been assaulted receives medical attention.
  • Notify the OIG Hotline at (888) 546-8740.

The OIG will initiate an investigation when there is a physical assault causing harm or serious bodily injury or when there is a credible threat of imminent harm or serious bodily injury. Generally the OIG will not investigate inappropriate statements and behavior or allegations of workplace harassment and bullying.

For more information, refer to the OIG's fact sheet, contact your management, and/or consult EPA Orders 1400.1 A2 and 4711. For more information about the EPA OIG in general:

  • Visit the OIG website.
  • Subscribe to our OIG email updates.
  • Learn more about our OIG Hotline.
  • Follow us on Twitter @EPAoig.

Free Cholesterol Screening

Posted Sept. 5

According to the American Heart Association, high cholesterol can lead to cardiovascular diseases, such as heart disease, heart attack, stroke, high blood pressure, and heart failure. One of the best ways to prevent cardiovascular disease is by getting your cholesterol checked. Call your nearest health unit for more information or to schedule an appointment:

  • Ronald Reagan Building Health Unit: 202-565-1940, Room G1-20.
    • Cholesterol screenings are available Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays from 8:30 to 10:30 a.m., by appointment only.
  • Potomac Yard Health Unit: 703-308-8574, Room S-1400.
    • Cholesterol screenings are available Monday through Thursday from 8:30 to 11:30 a.m., by appointment only.

The health unit nurses will be using a Cholestech machine, a high-tech device that screens cholesterol with only a finger stick.

Screenings are available to EPA federal employees only. This event is presented to support health and wellness. The EPA does not endorse this business, practitioner, or service.

Take Care and Be Prepared

Posted Aug. 30

Knowing how to respond to an emergency can help you stay safe if one occurs. To prepare for emergencies:

  • Know your occupant emergency and shelter-in-place plans.
  • Create a personal emergency plan and review it with your family.
  • Keep an emergency kit at work and at home.
  • Visit the Federal Emergency Management Agency website to learn about the 2018 National Preparedness Month weekly themes and the FEMA text message program.

In the event of an emergency, call 911, the security command post at 202-564-2205, and your health unit. For more information, visit EPA's Preparedness Month page.

Don't Wait to Vaccinate

Posted Aug. 29

Each year, thousands of American adults become seriously ill and are hospitalized due to vaccine-preventable diseases, according to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention. A visit to your personal physician for an immunization can protect against the flu, shingles, pneumonia in addition to other diseases. Getting vaccinated not only protects you, but those around you who are more susceptible to illness, such as older adults, infants, and those with an immunodeficiency.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
