EZ Desktop Records Tool Now Available

Posted July 2

Have you noticed the two new icons on your desktop? These icons are part of a new, easy EZ Desktop Records tool to save your electronic files as official agency records! It's as simple as dragging and dropping your file into one of the two new icons on your desktop: "EZ Desktop Records - Shared" or "EZ Desktop Records - Private." Please note, the EZ Desktop Records icons will only appear if you have already synced your files to OneDrive.

Learn how to sync your files to OneDrive and more about this new tool on the EZ Desktop Records intranet.

Nominations Being Accepted for CXO Fellowship Program

Posted June 26

Nominations are now being accepted for the 2018-2019 CXO Fellowship until July 31. The mission of the CXO Fellowship is to invest in outstanding federal employees (GS 9-14) by broadening their knowledge of government management functions and foster cross-government relationships through interactive discussions and collaboration. CXO Fellows will attend a unique set of educational seminars and events sponsored by the Office of Executive Councils. There is no cost for the program and Fellows remain in their current position while participating in the year-long program. Fellows must be stationed in the Washington, DC region. All interested applicants must be approved and nominated by their AAship (OARM, OCFO, or OEI).

Health & Wellness

Step Up and Take the Stairs

Posted June 22

Taking the stairs is a simple way to add physical activity to your daily routine. In fact, walking up stairs burns almost five times more calories than taking the elevator or escalator. Whether taking the stairs while at work, at home, or in your free time, step up your activity level with these tips:

  • Download the Step Up and Take the Stairs tracking log to set and achieve goals for taking the stairs more often each month.
  • Post the updated Take the Stairs posters around your workplace to motivate others to burn calories, not kilowatts.
  • Exit the elevator a few levels above or below your floor and take the stairs the rest of the way.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle.
  • If you are only traveling three or fewer floors up or down, save the elevator for longer trips and take the stairs instead.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

New Access Procedures at WJC Buildings

Posted July 13

Starting July 23, EPA HQ Security will begin a 30-day employee testing phase of all new building entry access control devices at all WJC building lobbies and employee entrances.

During this 30-day period, employees are encouraged, but not required, to try using their EPASS badge at any turnstile and/or employee entrance badge reader location at the WJC buildings or the EPA RRB tower to become more familiar with the new system and verify that their EPASS badge works. Physical Security Officers will not deny employees from entering if their badge does not work during this testing period.

Access/entry control via the EPASS badge and access control devices will be enforced starting August 20. Therefore, we encourage everyone to test their badges during the trial period to avoid any entry delays.

For more information including testing instructions and new badge holders, contact OARM-OA-HQ-OB@epa.gov or the Facilities Management and Services Division HQ Building Services Desk at 202-564-2100.
