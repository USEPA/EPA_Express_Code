Defend Against Diabetes

Posted April 3

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, more than 30 million Americans are currently living with diabetes, and one in four cases is undiagnosed. Diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels, which increases the risk of developing heart disease or having a stroke.

Visit the American Diabetes Association website to take an online diabetes risk assessment for type 2 diabetes (Exit). If you score "at risk" for type 2 diabetes, follow up with your personal physician to schedule a blood glucose screening, and take steps to eat a balanced diet and maintain a healthy weight. You should also visit your personal physician at least once a year to monitor your health.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Help Spot Heart Attacks

Posted March 7

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, approximately 735,000 Americans have a heart attack every year. Recognizing the signs and symptoms of a heart attack is the best way to prevent cardiac death. Signs and symptoms include the following:

  • Pain or discomfort in the chest or other areas of the upper body (i.e., one or both arms, back, neck, jaw or stomach).
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Feeling weak, lightheaded or faint.
  • Unexplained or unusual tiredness.

Call 911 immediately if you or someone around you experiences these symptoms.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Learn the Warning Signs of a Stroke

Posted March 4

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, strokes are the fifth leading cause of death in the United States and are a major cause of adult disability. Most strokes occur when blood clots or other particles block the blood vessels to the brain.

Common warning signs and symptoms of a stroke are:

  • Sudden trouble talking or walking.
  • Sudden confusion.
  • Sudden dizziness, or loss of balance or coordination.
  • Sudden numbness in the face, arm or leg, especially on one side of the body.
  • Severe headache with no known cause.

Call 911 immediately if you or someone around you experiences these symptoms.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
