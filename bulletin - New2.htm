WJC-North Active Shooter Response Drill

Posted March 15

Where: WJC-North, 5th floor only
When: Thursday, April 26 from 2-4 p.m.
What: A drill designed to allow EPA personnel to increase their proficiency in Active Shooter Response through the use of role players and simulated gunfire.
How do I respond: If you are a drill participant, use the RUN. HIDE. FIGHT. options to respond. Although drill sound effects may be audible on adjacent floors, if you are not participating in the drill, please continue business as usual and avoid travel to the William Jefferson Clinton North 5th floor. Active Shooter Response information can be found on the intranet at http://goo.gl/QsXrzs. Questions or comments should be directed to Bill Bellerose at Bellerose.William@epa.gov or 202-566-2191.

In preparation for the William Jefferson Clinton North (WJC-N) Active Shooter Response Drill, scheduled for April 26, 2018, three training sessions are available for drill participants to learn about the RUN.HIDE.FIGHT. response options:

  • April 17, 11 a.m., WJC-N 5530
  • April 19, 1:30 p.m., WJC-N 6530
  • April 23, 2 p.m., WJC-N 4530

Call to Action for a Learning Reaction: Host a New AAAS Fellow

Posted March 19

Is your office interested in hosting a highly qualified scientist or engineer to learn about the role of science and policy in government? With a few simple steps, you can be part of the upcoming interviews with American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) Science and Technology Policy Fellowship (STPF) fellows. Through EPA’s AAAS and STPF program, your office can support and mentor new AAAS STPF fellows through learning opportunities while benefitting from the unique knowledge and expertise that each person brings to EPA. All you need to do is submit a completed placement description form to Jayne Michaud by March 23. After, you will receive more information and instructions on participation in the interviews held in April.

EPA Leave Bank Election Form (3160-6) Is Now Electronic

Posted March 1

The Leave Bank Election Form, EPA Form 3160-6, is now electronic! Employees must use EPA Form 3160-6 to join the Leave Bank during the annual Open Season or within 30 days of becoming a new employee of the EPA. Additionally, employees who are Leave Bank members and non-members can also use this form to donate leave to a specific employee or to donate hours to the leave bank.

Employees can visit http://intranet.epa.gov/ohr/benefits/leavebank/ for more information on the National Leave Bank Program or contact your Local Leave Bank Program Manager.

Health & Wellness

Eat Healthy, Be Healthy

Posted March 5

Healthy food choices and proper nutrition can help prevent obesity, type 2 diabetes, heart disease, cancer, and osteoporosis. To get in the habit of maintaining healthy food choices:

  • Select healthy vending machine options, marked by a "yellow tag for a healthier bag."
  • Bring healthy, low-fat foods to meetings.
  • Explore the healthy food choices at your local snack bar.
  • Visit the Capital Harvest (i.e., the local farmers’ market) at the Ronald Reagan Building and International Trade Center on Fridays from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m., May through November.
  • Join the Walk to Wellness and Step It Up Challenge to commit to healthy eating, regular physical activity and lifelong good habits.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Join the Headquarters Step It Up Challenge Team

The EPA Step It Up Challenge is back - join the EPA Headquarters teamto step up your fitness and earn points for our team by tracking daily steps and minutes of exercise. From Feb. 1 to March 31, 2018, headquarters will compete against other large EPA locations (500 or more employees). This year’s challenge also has a few new features:

  • The challenge will have a monthly format, with scores re-setting at the end of each month. This will allow for winners and rankings for both February and March.
  • Each month, participants can complete a wellness challenge to earn bonus points.
  • If you signed up for the challenge in 2017, you do not need to register again in 2018 to participate - just start logging your steps and exercise beginning on Feb. 1.
      • The online reporting form will have an option to retrieve your password if you have forgotten it.

To learn more and sign up for the challenge, visit the EPA intranet or review the Participants Fact Sheet.
