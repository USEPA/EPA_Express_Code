Get Your New EPASS Badge

Posted Oct. 30

All EPA personnel must have a new EPASS badge by Dec. 31; the new EPASS badges say, "EPA." After this date, your existing badge, which says,"US EPA," will no longer provide building access.

After receiving an email from HSPD12Admin notifying you to get enrolled for a new badge, don't wait. Follow the steps below to receive your EPASS badge:

  • Make an appointment at your local badging office, or visit one of the 200 USAccess enrollment sites near your work or home.
  • Bring two forms of ID with you to your appointment (driver's license and current EPASS badge are acceptable).
  • You will be notified when your badge is ready and directed where to pick it up.

If you have any questions, contact SmartCard@epa.gov or your local badging office.

New Contract for Sign Language Interpreting and Captioning Services

Posted Oct. 25

EPA awarded a new contract for sign language interpreting and Communication Access Real-Time Translation (CART)/captioning services to Access Interpreting. The Office of Human Resources centrally manages sign language interpreting and CART/captioning services for use by deaf and hard of hearing employees at EPA headquarters and for use by any headquarters program office to support internal or public events nationwide.

Regional offices can use the contract, which was competed and awarded as a blanket purchase agreement (BPA No.68HEOH18A0005) to obtain services to support their employees and to support their internal and public events at their discretion and at their own costs. However, the use of this contract by regional offices is not mandatory.

Access Interpreting offers the following services as part of the BPA:

  • Basic sign language.
  • Scientific sign language.
  • Video remote interpreting.
  • Computer access real-time translation (on-site and remote).

Additional information on the new sign language interpreting and CART/captioning contract can be found on the EPA intranet's Interpreter site or by emailing the OHR Interpreting mailbox at interpretingservices@epa.gov.
