Osteoporosis Screening

Posted April 16

Dates: Tuesday April 30 (by appointment only)
Location: Ronald Reagan Health Unit Room G1-20WJCS 2138

Osteoporosis is a disease that causes bones to become thinner, more fragile, and more likely to break. Developing osteoporosis puts you at risk for fractures that can result in shrinking height, back pain or a humped back.

For help determining your risk, register for a screening on Tuesday, April 30, 2019. Screenings are available by appointment only and cost $35. Call American Screening at 800-686-3035 or email amscreening@msn.com to schedule your appointment.

Screenings are available to EPA federal employees only. This event is presented to support health and wellness. EPA does not endorse this business, practitioner or service.

Be Your Best Without Stress

Posted April 15

Stress is a physical, emotional and behavioral reaction to change in a person's life. According to the Mayo Clinic, long-term stress can increase the risk of developing health problems, such as depression, heart disease, weight gain, and anxiety. To help combat stress:

  • Reflect on what you have accomplished at the end of the day instead of focusing on what you were unable to do.
  • Use effective planning to minimize the impact of potentially stressful situations.
  • Minimize contact with individuals who cause stress when possible.
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle by eating a balanced diet, getting enough sleep, and exercising regularly.
  • Take time to relax.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Sleep Well

Posted April 4

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, one-third of Americans report that they usually get less than the recommended amount of sleep. Insufficient sleep is linked to health complications, including chronic diseases such as diabetes, Heart disease, obesity, and depression.

  • Go to bed earlier to get at least seven hours of sleep.
  • Ensure that your bedroom is quiet, dark, and comfortable.
  • Avoid large meals and alcohol prior to going to sleep.
  • Reduce your caffeine intake, especially later in the day.
  • Manage your stress levels.
  • Avoid rigorous activities (e.g., exercising) in the evening.
  • Use a nighttime setting for your smartphone and other electronic devices before bed.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Defend Against Diabetes

Posted April 3

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, more than 30 million Americans are currently living with diabetes, and one in four cases is undiagnosed. Diabetes is a chronic disease characterized by elevated blood glucose levels, which increases the risk of developing heart disease or having a stroke.

Visit the American Diabetes Association website to take an online diabetes risk assessment for type 2 diabetes (Exit). If you score "at risk" for type 2 diabetes, follow up with your personal physician to schedule a blood glucose screening, and take steps to eat a balanced diet and maintain a healthy weight. You should also visit your personal physician at least once a year to monitor your health.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
