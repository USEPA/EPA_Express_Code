UPDATED: Upcoming Accessibility Webinars

Posted Sept. 26

Below is information on upcoming accessibility webinars:

Introduction to PDF Accessibility
When: Thursday, Oct. 11 from 1-3 p.m.
Description: The Introduction to PDF Accessibility course provides information to guide EPA employees as they develop source files that will become accessible PDFs. It covers foundational concepts regarding PDFs and best practices when creating source files in general and in specific Microsoft Office applications before being converted into a Section 508 compliant PDF.

Advanced Accessibility for PDFs
When: Thursday, Oct. 25 from 1-3 p.m.
Description: The Advanced Accessibility for PDFs course covers aspects of a PDF that affect the requirements for making it Section 508 compliant and emphasizes the importance of the Section 508 compliance of the source file in making a PDF version compliant. The course then provides a brief overview of aspects of the source file to check for common formats including MS Word, PowerPoint, and Excel and presents a process for making a PDF compliant. This course does not provide training on how to use Adobe Acrobat Professional and the Acrobat features and tools required to make a PDF file Section 508 compliant.

Feds Feed Families: There's Still Time to Be a Hall of Famer

Posted Sept. 24

The Feds Feed Families Hall of Fame provides special recognition to those who donate at certain levels. There are still a few weeks left of this year's campaign, so you still have time to donate enough to join the hall of fame! You can participate in the hall of fame challenge as an individual or as a group. Groups may include as many as five people. You can be recognized at one of three levels depending on the amount of food you donate. For example, a group of EPA employees attended a farm gleaning event that produced 6,000 pounds of produce. Individually, each employee was attributed with 350 pounds, which automatically makes them silver level hall of famers. Learn more about the Hall of Fame, find your office's FFF coordinator, and learn about all the ways you can donate on the Feds Feed Families intranet site.

Health & Wellness

Protect Your Prostate

Posted Sept. 20

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, prostate cancer is the most common non-skin cancer among American men. Risk factors include age and a family history of prostate cancer.

Symptoms commonly include:

  • Difficulty starting to urinate or weak and/or interrupted flow.
  • Frequent urination, especially at night.
  • Difficulty emptying the bladder completely.
  • Pain or burning during urination
  • Blood in urine or semen.
  • Pain in the back, hips, or pelvis that does not go away.

Talk to your personal physician if you experience these symptoms or are concerned about changes related to your prostate.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Avoid Developing Arthritis

Posted Sept. 19

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, arthritis affects more than one in four American adults. The CDC also reports that arthritis is a major cause of work disability and one of the most common chronic conditions in the United States.

Arthritis describes more than 100 conditions that affect the joints or connective tissues around the joints. Symptoms commonly include pain and stiffness in and around one or more joints.

To prevent developing arthritis:

  • Incorporate regular physical activity into your daily routine to increase or maintain muscle strength, which supports and protects joints.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Protect your joints from injury and overuse.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Free Cholesterol Screening

Posted Sept. 5

One of the best ways to prevent cardiovascular disease is by getting your cholesterol checked. Call your nearest health unit for more information or to schedule an appointment:

  • Ronald Reagan Building Health Unit: 202-565-1940, Room G1-20.
    • Cholesterol screenings are available Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays from 8:30 to 10:30 a.m., by appointment only.
  • Potomac Yard Health Unit: 703-308-8574, Room S-1400.
    • Cholesterol screenings are available Monday through Thursday from 8:30 to 11:30 a.m., by appointment only.

The health unit nurses will be using a Cholestech machine, a high-tech device that screens cholesterol with only a finger stick.

Screenings are available to EPA federal employees only. This event is presented to support health and wellness. The EPA does not endorse this business, practitioner, or service.
