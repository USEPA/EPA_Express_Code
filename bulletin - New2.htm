Martin Luther King Day Jr. Celebration

Posted Feb. 15

Date: February 27
Time: 3:30-4:30 p.m.
Location: In person: Green Room; Remote: EPAtv

EPA will hold a celebration in honor of Martin Luther King Jr. Day. This celebration was postponed from January due to the government shutdown.

The keynote speaker is Ms. Willie King. Ms. King (no blood relation) was MLK's secretary during the Civil Rights Movement. Ms. King traveled the country by MLK's side to advocate for the fair and equal treatment of all people. She was also tasked with typing up MLK's handwritten notes that eventually became his famous letter written while he was confined in a Birmingham jail.

The MC is Office of Civil Rights Director, Vicki Simons.

EPAtv information: Live streaming will be available via EPAtv. A recording of this event will be available February 28 via the EPAtv-on-demand site. Please remember that EPAtv is only available through Internet Explorer while on the EPA network and cannot be accessed remotely.

The AAAS and ASPPH Fellowship Year Begins Fall 2019

Posted Feb. 12

Bring fresh ideas and unique expertise to your EPA team by supporting Fellows from the the American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS) and the Association of Schools and Programs of Public Health (ASPPH). EPA's partnerships with the AAAS and ASPPH fellowship programs provide highly qualified professionals with real-world opportunities to apply their knowledge while learning first-hand about policymaking and implementation at the federal level.

AAAS Science & Technology Policy Fellows (placed in headquarters only) range from early career to experienced scientists, engineers, and other professionals who hold doctoral degrees. ASPPH Environmental Health Fellows (can be placed in any EPA program and regional office) are recent masters and doctoral graduates from schools and programs accredited by the Council on Education for Public Health. Interested EPA host offices agree to fund and mentor their Fellow for one year with the possibility of renewing for a second year. EPA placement descriptions are due by March 7. For more information about how to participate, please contact Jayne Michaud at michaud.jayne@epa.gov.

Reduce Your Radon Risk

Posted Feb. 14

Radon is a naturally occurring, odorless gas that can damage your lungs when inhaled over time. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, radon gas exposure is the second leading cause of lung cancer after smoking. For non-smokers, radon exposure is the leading cause of lung cancer.

Radon gas can enter homes and buildings through cracks in floors, walls and foundations. The EPA recommends testing homes for radon and reducing any elevated levels detected (i.e., 4 picocuries per liter or more). If a test shows above-acceptable levels, there are several methods for reducing radon levels in your home such as installing a home ventilation pipe system and fan.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Exercises at Your Desk

Posted Feb. 11

Take a short exercise break during your workday to stay limber, increase productivity, and improve your health. Perform resistance band exercises to work multiple muscle groups at once and increase your coordination. Pilates, strength training, core, and other exercises can increase your flexibility, improve your posture, and maintain a healthy heart. Visit the Exercises at Your Desk page to view videos and get started exercising at your desk.

If you do not have a resistance band and want to try these exercises at your desk, contact your wellness coordinator, SeVera Wilson, at wilson.severa@epa.gov to borrow one.
