Negotiate Better: An Interest-Based Approach

Posted June 18

When: July 18 at 9 a.m. to 4:30 p.m. and July 19 at 9 a.m. to 12:30 p.m.
Room: WJCS 2138

Learn to use an interest-based approach to negotiation and improve your chances of reaching lasting agreements. On the first day of this interactive training, you will learn practical negotiation techniques and engage in exercises to prepare you to negotiate. On the second day, you will practice your skills in a multi-party negotiation simulation.

If you want to improve your ability to negotiate successfully, this course is for you. Whether the negotiations are with internal or external stakeholders, including states, communities, other federal agencies, industry groups, or even outside of work, this training will help. Also, this training is FREE!

Upcoming Accessiblity Webinar

Posted May 30

Advanced Accessibility for PDFs

  • When: June 21 from 1-3 p.m.
  • Description: The Advanced Accessibility for PDFs course covers aspects of a PDF that affect the requirements for making it Section 508 compliant and emphasizes the importance of the Section 508 compliance of the source file in making a PDF version compliant. The course then provides a brief overview of aspects of the source file to check for common formats, including MS Word, PowerPoint, and Excel and presents a process for making a PDF compliant. This course does not provide training on how to use Adobe Acrobat Professional and the Acrobat features and tools required to make a PDF file Section 508 compliant.

Get Certified to Save a Life

Posted June 14

According to the American Heart Association, almost 90 percent of individuals who suffer cardiac arrest outside of hospitals do not survive, but those that receive immediate cardiopulmonary resuscitation can double or triple their chance of survival. In addition, three out of four sudden cardiac arrest victims could be saved if trained individuals use an automated external defibrillator on the victims within three minutes.

At headquarters, the EPA regularly offers a training class that covers AED use, CPR, first aid, emergency oxygen administration and bloodborne pathogens. To register for a class or recertify, please email AED_Program@epa.gov. For questions, contact SeVera Wilson or 202-564-0097.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Stay Safe on the Job

Posted May 25

EPA has one of the lowest injury and illness rates in the federal government, but it is always striving to improve. To protect your health and keep yourself and others safe from potential workplace hazards:

  • Review health and safety guidelines relevant to your job or task.
  • Stay up-to-date on all health and safety training required for your job.
  • Prevent slips, trips and falls by keeping your workspace clean and free of clutter..
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle.
  • Familiarize yourself with the occupant emergency plan for headquarters.
  • Report all work-related injuries and illnesses to your Safety, Health and Environmental Program Manager, Shuan Barbour-Swann , or call the EPA Injury and Illness Reporting Hotline at 877-301-SAFE (7233).

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.
