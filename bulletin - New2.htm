Workshop on Environmental Justice

Posted July 18

When: August 3 from 11 a.m. to 1 p.m.
Where: WJCS Room 6226 or Adobe Connect

The Office of Environmental Justice (OEJ) is hosting a 2 hour workshop for summer law clerks and interns. The workshop will cover the principles of environmental justice, the evolution of the movement, and the efforts taken by the EPA to ensure the fair treatment and meaningful involvement of all people regardless of race, color, national origin, or income, with respect to the development, implementation, and enforcement of environmental laws, regulations, and policies.

Participants will hear from a range of speakers within OEJ on topics such as the history of the EJ movement, a legal perspective, and tools used to identify vulnerable communities. Speakers and agenda will be shared the week of the agenda. You do not need to register, but feel free to contact Simone Walter if you have any questions.

Workshop Goals:

  • Inform participants about the programs of the Office of Environmental Justice.
  • Foster dialogue for participants to expand their understanding of environmental justice.
  • Gain insight on how the EPA contributes to the environmental justice movement.
  • Explore the intersections of environmental justice in different public and private sectors.

Nominations Being Accepted for CXO Fellowship Program

Posted June 26

Nominations are now being accepted for the 2018-2019 CXO Fellowship until July 31. The mission of the CXO Fellowship is to invest in outstanding federal employees (GS 9-14) by broadening their knowledge of government management functions and foster cross-government relationships through interactive discussions and collaboration. CXO Fellows will attend a unique set of educational seminars and events sponsored by the Office of Executive Councils. Help Your Hearing

Posted July 19

According to the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health, hearing loss is the third most common chronic physical condition for American adults. Protect against noise-induced hearing loss by limiting your exposure to loud sounds:

  • Keep the volume low when listening to music, TV, or a radio, especially with headphones.
  • Take breaks from loud noises.
  • Use quieter products (e.g., power tools and recreational vehicles) when possible.
  • Maintain equipment (lawn care equipment or power tools)
    • to reduce noise caused by worn, loose, or unbalanced machine parts.
  • Wear earplugs when using loud equipment or at loud, noisy activities, or places (e.g., concerts and sporting events).
  • Avoid the loudest sound-producing source (i.e., loudspeakers) at public events.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

New Access Procedures at WJC Buildings

Posted July 13

Starting July 23, EPA HQ Security will begin a 30-day employee testing phase of all new building entry access control devices at all WJC building lobbies and employee entrances.

During this 30-day period, employees are encouraged, but not required, to try using their EPASS badge at any turnstile and/or employee entrance badge reader location at the WJC buildings or the EPA RRB tower to become more familiar with the new system and verify that their EPASS badge works. Physical Security Officers will not deny employees from entering if their badge does not work during this testing period.

Access/entry control via the EPASS badge and access control devices will be enforced starting August 20. Therefore, we encourage everyone to test their badges during the trial period to avoid any entry delays.

For more information including testing instructions and new badge holders, contact OARM-OA-HQ-OB@epa.gov or the Facilities Management and Services Division HQ Building Services Desk at 202-564-2100.
