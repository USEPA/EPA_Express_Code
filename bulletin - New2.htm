Winter Driving Tips

Posted Dec. 18

Driving in the winter can be challenging, especially in snowy or icy weather conditions. In addition to avoiding driver distractions, remember these safety tips:

  • Maintain good vehicle operating conditions.
  • Always keep your gas tank at least half full.
  • Know the weather and traffic reports before you head out.
  • Plan your route before you leave and tell someone when you expect to arrive.
  • Drive slowly and keep a safe distance from other vehicles.
  • Keep your cellphone charged.
  • Carry an emergency kit.
  • When stranded, stay put and do not overexert yourself.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Ready to Quit Smoking? Start Today!

Posted Dec. 12

Quitting smoking is one of the most important steps you can take to improve your health. Tobacco use can cause serious health problems, and secondhand smoke can be harmful to non-smokers. As part of its smoking policy, the EPA prohibits the use of tobacco products and electronic cigarettes (i.e., e-cigarettes) indoors and outdoors (i.e., within 25 feet of EPA facility or garage entrances). To help you quit smoking, check out these resources:

  • Join the EPA Headquarters' Smoking Cessation Program.
  • Discover effective treatments and resources through the Federal Employees Health Benefits Program or your health insurance plan.
  • Call 800-QUIT-NOW (800-784-8669) to speak with smoking cessation counselors in your state.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet and Smokefree.gov.

Stay a Step Ahead of Slips, Trips, and Falls

Posted Dec. 4

Based on data reported by EPA employees, slips, trips and falls make up the number one category of work-related injuries and illnesses at the EPA. Consider these tips to avoid slips, trips and falls at work:

  • Wear sturdy footwear appropriate for your tasks.
  • Walk in well-lit areas or turn on lights when needed.
  • Clean up office clutter and properly store or cover computer cords.
  • Report spills right away to the Building Services Desk at 202-564-2100.
  • Pay attention while walking and avoid multitasking as much as possible.

If you suffer an injury or illness at work, report it to your supervisor and the EPA Headquarters SHEMP manager, Shuan Barbour-Swann at 202-564-1650, or call the EPA Injury and Illness Reporting Hotline at 877-301-SAFE (7233).

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Properly Position Your Workstation

Posted Dec. 3

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, musculoskeletal disorders (e.g., carpal tunnel syndrome and muscle strains) account for one-third of all injuries and illnesses at work in the United States. To prevent injury and reduce the risk for musculoskeletal disorders:

  • Sit in a relaxed, neutral body position.
  • Use wrist rests while typing.
  • Lift and carry loads close to the body.
  • Arrange your workstation equipment (e.g., computer monitor, keyboard and telephone) to minimize physical stress.

To request an ergonomics assessment at headquarters, visit the Headquarters Onsite Ergonomics Assessments page.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Healthy Holiday Eating

Posted Nov. 29

Being more mindful of your eating habits can help you be healthier and stay on track with your health and fitness goals during the holiday season. Consider the following tips to eat healthy during the holidays:

  • If you have diabetes, eat as close to your usual meal times as possible to maintain normal blood glucose levels.
  • If you are invited to a holiday party, offer to bring a healthy dish. Prepare your own healthy snacks to eat if the meal is not ready by your regular meal time
  • Eat smaller portions of festive snacks and desserts and eat only when you are hungry.
  • Start with vegetables or fruits to reduce your appetite.
  • Slow down while eating and enjoy each bite to help give your body enough time to tell your brain that you are satisfied.
  • Maintain a food diary to track what and how much you eat during the holiday season.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Hello Handwashing, Goodbye Germs

Posted Nov. 28

Handwashing is the simplest way to prevent the spread of germs, disease, and illness. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, the proper handwashing technique is as follows:

  • Wet hands with clean, running water and apply soap.
  • Lather your hands by rubbing them with soap on all surfaces.
  • Scrub your hands for at least 20 seconds.
  • Rinse hands well under clean, running water.
  • Dry hands using a clean towel or air dryer.

Alternatively, you can use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer containing at least 60 percent alcohol to keep your hands clean.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
