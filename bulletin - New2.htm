New Mobile Version of EJSCREEN Easier to Use in Communities!

Posted April 24

EPA has launched a new mobile version of EJSCREEN, the agency's nationally acclaimed environmental justice (EJ) screening and mapping tool. EJSCREEN was built for EPA staff to analyze environmental and demographic factors while developing programs and policies that impact overburdened communities. It was originally built for use on standard desktop and laptop computers, which are not always accessible to stakeholders working in communities. Since releasing EJSCREEN to the public, stakeholder feedback showed the tool could be friendlier with mobile devices, and EPA website data showed 50 percent of users accessed EJSCREEN from a mobile device.

This new mobile version of EJSCREEN will make the tool more accessible to our partners, communities, and anyone interested in environmental justice. To access the mobile version, visit EJSCREEN on a tablet or mobile phone or check out the EJSCREEN website for more information. You can also get the latest information about EJSCREEN and other EJ activities on the EJ blog.

Released: 2018 Environmental Justice Progress Report

Posted April 19

The Office of Environmental Justice (OEJ) released the annual Environmental Justice Progress Report for FY2017 on April 19. The report commemorates the 25th anniversary of OEJ and the accomplishments highlighted in the report affirm through action how, after a quarter century of progress, environmental justice (EJ) is deeply ingrained in EPA’s fabric. An overarching focus of the report is demonstrating tangible results in minority, low-income, tribal and indigenous communities.

These results highlighted are only a few of the many accomplishments from 2017. Many of the examples required decades of effort, and are a testament to the long-standing commitment, innovation and hard work of the EPA staff who do this work on a day-to-day basis. Thank you for your hard work in FY17 and we look forward to seeing what you continue to do for environmental justice this year!

Health & Wellness

Beat High Blood Pressure

Posted May 1

According to the American Heart Association, nearly half of American adults have high blood pressure, which is defined as 130 over 80 per new guidelines as of November 2017. In addition, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reports that high blood pressure can cause heart disease and stroke, which are leading causes of death in the United States.

Reduce your risk by:

  • Eating a healthy diet
  • Exercising regularly
  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Managing stress
  • Not smoking
  • Limiting alcohol use
  • Getting your blood pressure checked regularly and addressing elevated blood pressure levels as prescribed by your personal physician

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.
