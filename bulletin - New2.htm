Breastfeeding Your Baby

Posted Aug. 2

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, nearly 52 percent of American women continue to breastfeed their babies six months after birth. The American Academy of Pediatrics recommends that mothers continue to breastfeed through the first year after birth and use supplemental solid food. Breastfed babies are less likely to have diarrhea, ear infections, or pneumonia; develop asthma or allergies; or become obese. Mothers who breastfeed have a decreased risk of breast and ovarian cancers.

The Headquarters Maternal Wellness Program offers regular prenatal breastfeeding classes and Worksite Brown Bag Lunch Seminars on topics such as weaning and pumping best practices. For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Choose Healthy Recipes

Posted July 23

Choosing healthy recipes will help you maintain a balanced diet. When selecting a healthy recipe, consider:

  • Looking for heart-healthy, low-calorie recipes that are low in saturated fat, cholesterol and sodium.
  • Trying a variety of foods, such as lean meats and vegetables.
  • Adding flavor with herbs and spices.
  • Selecting unprocessed foods such as fresh produce, raw nuts, beans, and seeds.
  • Visiting a farmers' market to pick up fresh produce.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Help Your Hearing

Posted July 19

According to the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health, hearing loss is the third most common chronic physical condition for American adults. Protect against noise-induced hearing loss by limiting your exposure to loud sounds:

  • Keep the volume low when listening to music, TV, or a radio, especially with headphones.
  • Take breaks from loud noises.
  • Use quieter products (e.g., power tools and recreational vehicles) when possible.
  • Maintain equipment (lawn care equipment or power tools) to reduce noise caused by worn, loose, or unbalanced machine parts.
  • Wear earplugs when using loud equipment or at loud, noisy activities, or places (e.g., concerts and sporting events).
  • Avoid the loudest sound-producing source (i.e., loudspeakers) at public events.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

New Access Procedures at WJC Buildings

Posted July 13

Starting July 23, EPA HQ Security will begin a 30-day employee testing phase of all new building entry access control devices at all WJC building lobbies and employee entrances.

During this 30-day period, employees are encouraged, but not required, to try using their EPASS badge at any turnstile and/or employee entrance badge reader location at the WJC buildings or the EPA RRB tower to become more familiar with the new system and verify that their EPASS badge works. Physical Security Officers will not deny employees from entering if their badge does not work during this testing period.

Access/entry control via the EPASS badge and access control devices will be enforced starting August 20. Therefore, we encourage everyone to test their badges during the trial period to avoid any entry delays.

For more information including testing instructions and new badge holders, contact OARM-OA-HQ-OB@epa.gov or the Facilities Management and Services Division HQ Building Services Desk at 202-564-2100.
