Prescription Drug Take-Back Day

Posted April 5

On Wednesday, April 25 from 10 a.m. to 1 p.m., bring unwanted or expired pharmaceutical drugs to the Ronald Reagan Building Health Unit, room G1-20, or the Potomac Yard Health Unit, room S-1400, for safe, free, and anonymous disposal.

This event will be held in conjunction with the Drug Enforcement Administration’s National Prescription Drug Take-Back Initiative to prevent drug abuse and theft by ridding homes of expired or unwanted medications.

For additional information on this event, visit the EPA intranet.

Federal Inter-Agency Holocaust Remembrance Program

Posted April 3

EPA, along with many other federal agencies, will commemorate the Holocaust with the 25th Annual Federal Inter-Agency Holocaust Remembrance Program on Thursday, April 12 from 11 a.m. to 12:30 p.m. EDT at the Lincoln Theater in Washington, D.C. The 2018 theme is Bearing Witness - A Holocaust Survivor on Schindler’s List. The keynote speaker is Holocaust survivor Halina Silber.

Broadcast information:

  • The event will be broadcast live on EPAtv. Please remember that EPAtv is available using only Internet Explorer while on the EPA network and cannot be accessed remotely.
  • The event will be live-streamed by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) on YouTube Live.
  • Phone lines are not available for this event, but the video recording is expected to be available on EPAtv On-Demand on Friday April 13.

For additional information on this event, please contact Jannette Stewart at 202-564-0405 and Michael Kauffman at 202-564-4499.

Connect With the OIG!

Posted March 30

EPA’s Office of Inspector General (OIG) helps the agency more efficiently and cost-effectively protect the environment by performing independent audits and evaluations of EPA operations and programs and by investigating criminal activity and misconduct. The OIG’s final audit and evaluation reports - which include the OIG’s findings and recommendations and the agency’s responses - are published on the OIG's website. That means EPA employees can easily keep up to date with the OIG’s work. Here are other ways EPA employees can connect with the OIG:

  • Subscribe to the OIG email updates and receive notices when new reports, project notifications, and other information are posted.
  • Follow the OIG on Twitter @EPAoig for the latest news about independent #oversight of agency programs and operations.
  • Listen to the OIG podcasts for quick, engaging summaries of OIG projects (transcripts available).
  • Subscribe to and watch videos on the OIG YouTube channel, including congressional testimony by OIG leadership.

New EPA Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Posted April 2

Effective April 1, 2018, we have contracted with Espyr, an independent firm, to provide Employee Assistance Program services to EPA Headquarters employees and their families. Espyr has a solid reputation for providing exceptional services and outstanding customer service and support.

Espyr offers many services as part of the EPA’s EAP program including:

  • 24/7 access to FREE counseling and referral services.
  • A large variety of on-line resources and programs.
  • Work/Life services including no-cost consultations with attorneys, financial professionals, child and eldercare services specialists and others.

If you are an EPA Headquarters employee in need of EAP services, please call 888-635-3202. Additionally, EPA Headquarters employees can request EAP services online at: http://www.espyr.com (user name usepa).

The EPA also provides EAP information and resources on its intranet site.

Headquarters employees may direct questions regarding EAP services to the National EAP Coordinator Barbara Viney, at 202-564-7972 or at viney.barbara@epa.gov.

2017 CFC Closeout/EPA Campaign Wins Award

Posted March 23

The final 2017 CFC fundraising totals for EPA headquarters and agency-wide are $603,085 and $1,608,184 respectively. Participation was about 14% at headquarters and agency-wide. EPA maintained its contributions ranking amongst non-defense agencies since the last campaign.

OW, as lead office for the 2017 CFC, held the EPA CFC thank you/awards ceremony on Feb. 28 to thank EPA CFC team members and recognize special office and campaign worker efforts. Thanks to Jini Mohanty, EPA CFC Manager, and over 100 coordinators and keyworkers for their efforts.

On March 23, Benita Best-Wong, 2017 EPA CFC Vice Chair; Robert Stevens, OW Management and Operations Staff Director; and Jini Mohanty received certificates on behalf of the EPA CFC campaign for winning the National Capital Area CFC innovator’s contest for the agency that demonstrated the most innovative approach and creativity. EPA tied with the US Navy.
