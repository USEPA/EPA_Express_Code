Health & Wellness

Stay Safe on the Job

Posted May 25

EPA has one of the lowest injury and illness rates in the federal government, but it is always striving to improve. To protect your health and keep yourself and others safe from potential workplace hazards:

  • Review health and safety guidelines relevant to your job or task.
  • Stay up-to-date on all health and safety training required for your job.
  • Prevent slips, trips and falls by keeping your workspace clean and free of clutter..
  • Maintain a healthy lifestyle.
  • Familiarize yourself with the occupant emergency plan for headquarters.
  • Report all work-related injuries and illnesses to your Safety, Health and Environmental Program Manager, Shuan Barbour-Swann , or call the EPA Injury and Illness Reporting Hotline at 877-301-SAFE (7233).

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Posted May 18

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, 70 percent of adults who are 20 years of age and older are overweight or obese, and more than 17 percent of children 6 to 11 years of age are obese. Being at an unhealthy weight puts you at risk for chronic diseases and other health problems, such as diabetes and high blood pressure. To achieve and maintain a healthy weight:

  • Assess your weight by calculating your body mass index, body composition, and waist circumference.
  • Set realistic goals.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet.
  • Balance the number of calories you consume with the number of calories you burn.
  • Add regular physical activity to your daily routine.

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Take Care of Your Eyes

Posted May 14

According to the National Institutes of Health National Eye Institute, millions of Americans experience problems with their vision every year. To keep your eyes healthy, schedule comprehensive eye exams with your eye care professional every one to two years.

Keep your eyes healthy throughout your lifetime by:

  • Knowing your family's eye health history.
  • Eating a healthy diet that includes foods high in omega 3 fatty acids (e.g., salmon or chia seeds)
    • and maintaining a healthy weight.
  • Quitting smoking.
  • Wearing sunglasses that block ultraviolet A and B radiation.
  • Practicing workplace and home eye safety by wearing personal protective equipment (e.g., safety glasses).

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.

Beat High Blood Pressure

Posted May 1

According to the American Heart Association, nearly half of American adults have high blood pressure, which is defined as 130 over 80 per new guidelines as of November 2017. In addition, the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention reports that high blood pressure can cause heart disease and stroke, which are leading causes of death in the United States.

Reduce your risk by:

  • Eating a healthy diet
  • Exercising regularly
  • Maintaining a healthy weight
  • Managing stress
  • Not smoking
  • Limiting alcohol use
  • Getting your blood pressure checked regularly and addressing elevated blood pressure levels as prescribed by your personal physician

For more information, please visit the EPA intranet.
