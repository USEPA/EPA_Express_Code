Health & Wellness

Protect Your Prostate

Posted Sept. 20

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, prostate cancer is the most common non-skin cancer among American men. Risk factors include age and a family history of prostate cancer.

Symptoms commonly include:

  • Difficulty starting to urinate or weak and/or interrupted flow.
  • Frequent urination, especially at night.
  • Difficulty emptying the bladder completely.
  • Pain or burning during urination
  • Blood in urine or semen.
  • Pain in the back, hips, or pelvis that does not go away.

Talk to your personal physician if you experience these symptoms or are concerned about changes related to your prostate.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.

Avoid Developing Arthritis

Posted Sept. 19

According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, arthritis affects more than one in four American adults. The CDC also reports that arthritis is a major cause of work disability and one of the most common chronic conditions in the United States.

Arthritis describes more than 100 conditions that affect the joints or connective tissues around the joints. Symptoms commonly include pain and stiffness in and around one or more joints.

To prevent developing arthritis:

  • Incorporate regular physical activity into your daily routine to increase or maintain muscle strength, which supports and protects joints.
  • Maintain a healthy weight.
  • Protect your joints from injury and overuse.

For more information, visit the EPA intranet.
